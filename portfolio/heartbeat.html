Web Application Heartbeat

Overview

Appian has a unified user portal called Home. Home is where many aspects of an employee’s life live -- Marketing creates campaigns, Sales tracks customer relationships, Engineering pushes features, and all employees experience events like promotions, departures, and company-wide all-hand meetings through Home. Home was notably missing a way to track the status of external applications, such as Netsuite, Concur, and Salesforce. Often, reporting of these outages are handled through our system’s administrator, and have limited availability to our employee end users. I proposed creating an external application tracking app that would both solve this need and allow us to test our newly-available RPA technology.

Summary

Problem:

While many of Appian’s employees reap the benefits of a centralized work portal, many also suffer from lack of knowledge and reporting on external systems when there is maintenance or an unplanned outage.

Strategy:

Strategy here was more or less handed to us -- we had a known problem with a conceptually straightforward solution, and new technology DYING to be used for this.

Solution:

Using Appian’s newly-acquired RPA technology, we built a backend process that checks the public-facing cloud status page for the relevant page status details. New outages will create a Home post (think like a News Post on Facebook), and resolved outages will update the original post to indicate that the outage is resolved. Furthermore, employees can check a real-time dashboard for the current status, and subscribe for email updates about outages.

Result:

All Appian employees now have access to a real-time dashboard that increases proactive visibility about current cloud application outages, and email notifications if you want.
